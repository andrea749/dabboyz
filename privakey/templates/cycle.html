Vigenere Cipher

Ciphers

The Vigenere cipher is similar to the classic Caesarian shift but allows for multiple values of offset. The image below shows what each shift would look like.





The offsets are determined by a keyword chosen by the user. For example, if the keyword is "abc" then the set of offsets would be 1,2,3 and every third letter would be replaced by the next letter in the alphabet, every letter after those would be replaced by the letter 2 places above in the alphabet, and every letter after that would be replaced by the letter 3 places ahead. So, with a keyword "abc", "dadboyz" becomes "ecgcqba".
